What we believe

The question is more important than the answer

The guru-sishya tradition — teacher and student, question and insight — is the oldest and most powerful model of learning we know. Kelvi is built on that foundation.

01

Students who ask better questions become better thinkers

The shift from "how do I?" to "why does this work?" to "what if I changed it?" — that is the shift from procedural to mathematical thinking.

02

AI should develop thinking, not replace it

Every other AI gives answers. Kelvi never does. It responds to every message with a question that helps the student ask a better one next time.

03

A math mindset belongs to everyone

Not just to students in elite schools. Not just to those who can afford tutors. Kelvi is built with equity at its core.

The question ladder

Three types of questions. One direction.

Kelvi identifies every question a student asks and coaches them toward the next level. This is the heart of what we mean by a math mindset.

P
Procedural

The starting point. Every student begins here. Not the destination — just the door.

"How do I find the area?" · "What formula do I use?"
C
Conceptual

The target. Shows the student is thinking about the mathematics, not just the calculation.

"Why does dividing by 7 make sense here?" · "What does this expression mean?"
G
Generative

The highest level. Changing the problem to explore what happens. Thinking like a mathematician.

"What if the scale were different?" · "What would happen if there were three people?"
