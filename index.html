What makes Kelvi different

Kelvi guides. It doesn't take over.

Unlike other AI tools, Kelvi doesn't treat math as content to deliver and answers to check. Where most tools build dependence on hints, feedback loops, and green checkmarks, Kelvi is built on a different premise: the thinking is the math. Not the answer. Not the score. The reasoning.

Kelvi's AI doesn't jump straight to "correct" or "incorrect" or hand over solutions. Instead, it invites learners to reflect, clarify language, notice patterns, and discover their own next move.

Why withhold the answer? Because the moment you hand it over, the thinking stops. The answer is the end of the question. Kelvi stays in the middle — where the learning actually happens.

Frequently asked questions

Is Kelvi a tutor?›
No. Kelvi is a thinking partner, not a tutor. It never delivers lessons, explains procedures, or walks students through steps. Instead, it asks questions that help learners examine their own reasoning and discover their next move independently.
Does Kelvi give answers?›
Never. Kelvi's AI is governed by 15 principles that prevent it from confirming right or wrong, giving solutions, or reacting differently to correct and incorrect responses. It guides learners to validate their own reasoning.
What ages and levels is Kelvi for?›
Kelvi Family is designed for young learners ages 4–13 developing mathematical curiosity. Kelvi Student covers courses from Integrated Math through Linear Algebra, Differential Equations, and Discrete Math. Kelvi School works with any math classroom.
How is Kelvi different from Khan Academy or ChatGPT?›
Khan Academy delivers content and tracks mastery. ChatGPT answers questions. Kelvi does neither — it develops the learner's ability to ask better questions and reason independently. There are no lessons, no scores, no badges, and no answer-checking.
Is my data private?›
Yes. Kelvi does not sell or share user data. Student interactions are used only to support the learning experience. We are committed to building trust with families, teachers, and learners.
